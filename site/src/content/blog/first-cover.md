Picked the simplest song I could think of to get back into it. Bob Dylan's *Knockin' on Heaven's Door*. Three chords (G, D, Am/C), 4/4, around 68 BPM. Hard to mess up.

## Guitar Tones

**Rhythm:** DELUXE (Fender clean) on Bus 5. Drive at 5, volume maxed, bright on. Neck pickup, tone rolled back. Going for that warm, clean Strat blues sound. No amp sim drive — all the grit comes from the outboard. The HA73 and WA76 are doing the real work.

**Lead:** ANGEL (Mesa-style) on Bus 11. Pulled the drive way down to 1.5, treble to 3, presence to 3. Wanted it dark and controlled, not the usual high-gain Mesa thing. More like a thick, singing lead tone with the outboard adding body.

Switching between them: toggle Ch18's send from Bus 10 (rhythm, RACKAMP) to Bus 11 (lead, ANGEL). The amp sim is post-outboard on the monitoring buses, so the outboard chain stays the same either way.

## Vocal

Ran the vocal through the full outboard chain — HA73 A for color, WA76 A for transient control, Opto for leveling. Then added a DOUBLE/THICK on MX2 (the vocal mix matrix) with mix at 65%, spread at 100%. Gives it width without sounding obviously processed.

## Session Players

Logic provides bass and drums. Added a SUB octaver (MID range, oct1 70%, mix 50%) on MX6 to push the bass into the midrange — gives it more presence alongside the guitar without competing on the low end.

## Lessons

- Logic's input monitoring must be OFF during tracking. Monitor through the Wing (via matrices to Model 12) for zero latency.
- The Model 12 USB/MTR switch is easy to forget. If the Model 12 channels go silent, check that first.
- Ch25 (vocal tape return) and Ch17 (live vocal) both feed MX2. No double as long as Logic input monitoring is off.
